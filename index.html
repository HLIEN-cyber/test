About Me

I am a Distinguished Professor at Cyber School, where I serve as the Director of the Drama and Performance Research Lab. I hold a Ph.D. in Performance Studies from New York University, specializing in the intersection of theatre, culture, and performance theory.

My academic career spans over two decades, during which I have made significant contributions to the fields of performance studies, cultural policy, and theatre history. In 1997, I co-founded the Creative Society Theatre in Taipei, becoming one of the few knowledge-based theatre artists who combines both theatre directing and academic research.

My research focuses on bridging traditional and contemporary performance practices, exploring how cultural memory and identity are constructed through performance. I am particularly interested in star studies, gender performance, and the cultural creative industries.

Lecture Topics

Core Lecture Themes

Performance and Star Studies

In-depth analysis of actor and star performance, examining how celebrity culture intersects with performance theory. Topics include star discourse, performance analysis, and the construction of celebrity identity in contemporary culture.

Theatre History and Methodology

Comprehensive exploration of theatre history research methods, examining how we study and understand performance across different historical periods and cultural contexts.

Gender Performance and Cultural Identity

Critical examination of how gender is performed and constructed through cultural practices, analyzing the relationship between performance, identity, and social power structures.

Cultural Policy and Creative Industries

Analysis of cultural policy frameworks and their impact on performing arts, exploring strategies for developing sustainable cultural creative industries.

Digital Theatre and New Media

Investigation of how digital technologies are transforming performance practices, examining the intersection of theatre, technology, and new media in contemporary performance.
